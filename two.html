Coronavirus Pandemic

What Is A Coronavirus?

A coronavirus is a kind of common virus that causes mild, upper-respiratory tract illnesses. There are many types of human coronaviruses and most are not dangerous. The novel coronavirus is being referred to as COVID-19 where'CO' stands for corona, 'VI' for virus, 'D' for disease, and '19' for 2019. COVID-19 is caused by a coronavirus called SARS-CoV-2.

How It Spreads

It is thought to spread mainly from person to person, mainly through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby or possibly be inhaled into the lungs.

Cases

Worldwide there have been more than 4 million confirmed cases of Covid-19. The U.S. has been impacted the most by the virus with more than 1.3 million confirmed cases. Older people and people who have severe pre-existing medical conditions seem to be at higher risk for developing serious complications from COVID-19.
